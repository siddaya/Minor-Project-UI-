Dry Chili Cultivation and Properties

Soil Properties for Dry Chili Cultivation:

  • Dry chili thrives in well-drained soils with good fertility and high organic matter content.
  • The soil pH should be between 6.0 and 7.5 for optimum growth.
  • The soil should be loose and friable to promote root development and water infiltration.
  • Sandy loam soils are ideal for dry chili cultivation.

Cultivation Process for Dry Chili:

  • Dry chili is usually grown from seed, which is sown directly in the field.
  • The seedlings emerge in about 10-14 days, and transplanting is done when they are 6-8 weeks old.
  • The spacing between plants should be around 30-45 cm, depending on the variety and growing conditions.
  • Dry chili requires regular irrigation, especially during the flowering and fruiting stages.
  • Fertilization is necessary to promote vigorous growth and high yields.
  • Pest and disease management is critical to prevent damage to the crop.

Properties of Dry Chili:

  • Dry chili contains a compound called capsaicin, which is responsible for its pungency.
  • It is a good source of vitamin C, vitamin A, potassium, and dietary fiber.
  • Dry chili has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties and may help boost metabolism and improve heart health.
  • It is commonly used in spicy dishes, sauces, and marinades.

Ideal Soil Moisture for Best Production of Dry Chili:

  • Dry chili requires consistent moisture throughout the growing season.
  • The ideal soil moisture content for dry chili is around 50-75% of field capacity.
  • Over-watering or under-watering can lead to poor growth and yield.
  • Soil moisture levels should be monitored regularly and irrigation adjusted accordingly.
